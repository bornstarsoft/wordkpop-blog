In a troubling revelation, Gunzilla Games, the blockchain game studio co-founded by acclaimed director Neill Blomkamp, is facing serious allegations from former employees regarding unpaid wages. Reports indicate that some staff members have not received their salaries for several months, raising concerns about the company's financial practices and treatment of its workforce.

Paul Creamer, a former animator at Gunzilla, publicly shared his experience on LinkedIn, claiming he hasn't been compensated since October 2025. His post has sparked a wave of similar testimonies from other employees, highlighting a pattern of delayed payments and unfulfilled promises from upper management. This situation not only affects the livelihoods of those involved but also casts a shadow over the burgeoning blockchain gaming industry, which is already grappling with its own set of challenges.

As the gaming community watches closely, the question remains: what measures will be taken to ensure that employees are treated fairly and that such issues do not persist in the future? The unfolding events at Gunzilla Games could serve as a crucial lesson for both developers and players alike in the rapidly evolving landscape of blockchain gaming.
